Korean has **21 vowels** in modern Hangul: 10 basic vowels and 11 compound vowels. The basic vowels are **ㅏ, ㅑ, ㅓ, ㅕ, ㅗ, ㅛ, ㅜ, ㅠ, ㅡ, and ㅣ**. Compound vowels combine these strokes into forms such as **ㅐ, ㅔ, ㅘ, ㅝ, ㅢ, and ㅚ**. Vowels decide the visual shape of a syllable block because they tell the consonants whether to stack left-to-right, top-to-bottom, or in a mixed layout.

Start by learning the contrast between bright **ㅏ /a/**, darker **ㅓ /eo/**, rounded **ㅗ /o/**, and rounded **ㅜ /u/**. Then add the y-vowels ㅑ, ㅕ, ㅛ, and ㅠ, which begin with a short “y” glide. Compound vowels are easier once those basics are familiar: many are combinations of a simple vowel plus **ㅣ /i/** or a rounded glide.

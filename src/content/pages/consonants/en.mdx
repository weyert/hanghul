Korean has **19 consonants** in modern Hangul: 14 basic consonants and 5 tense consonants. The basic consonants are **ㄱ, ㄴ, ㄷ, ㄹ, ㅁ, ㅂ, ㅅ, ㅇ, ㅈ, ㅊ, ㅋ, ㅌ, ㅍ, and ㅎ**. The tense consonants are **ㄲ, ㄸ, ㅃ, ㅆ, and ㅉ**. Many consonant shapes were designed to hint at how the mouth, tongue, lips, or throat forms the sound.

For beginners, the most important pattern is the contrast between **plain**, **aspirated**, and **tense** sounds. Plain consonants such as ㄱ, ㄷ, ㅂ, and ㅈ are softer than their romanization may suggest. Aspirated consonants such as ㅋ, ㅌ, ㅍ, and ㅊ release a clear puff of air. Tense consonants such as ㄲ, ㄸ, ㅃ, ㅆ, and ㅉ are held with more pressure. Use the cards below to connect each letter shape with its sound, romanization, IPA value, and example word.

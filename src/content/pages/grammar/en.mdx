## 1. Sentence Structure: SOV

<SectionCard>

In English and Dutch, we usually say **Subject - Verb - Object** (e.g., "I drink water").
In Korean, the verb **always** comes at the very end: **Subject - Object - Verb**.

<div className="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-6 mt-4">
  <div className="rounded-xl p-4" style={{ background: 'var(--c-surface)', border: '1px solid var(--c-border)' }}>
    <p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-widest mb-2" style={{ color: 'var(--c-3)' }}>English (SVO)</p>
    <p>I <span style={{ color: 'var(--c-accent-text)' }}>drink</span> water.</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-xl p-4" style={{ background: 'var(--c-surface)', border: '1px solid rgba(99,102,241,0.3)' }}>
    <p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-widest mb-2" style={{ color: 'var(--c-accent-text)' }}>Korean (SOV)</p>
    <p className="korean-text">나 물 <span className="font-bold" style={{ color: 'var(--c-accent-text)' }}>마셔요</span>.</p>
    <p className="text-xs mt-1" style={{ color: 'var(--c-3)' }}>Na mul masyeoyo (I water drink)</p>
  </div>
</div>

*Pro tip: Since the verb is at the end, you have to listen to the very last word to know if someone is asking a question, stating a fact, or giving a command!*

</SectionCard>

## 2. Particles: The Language Glue

Because word order can sometimes be flexible in Korean, the language uses "particles" attached
to nouns to signal their role in the sentence.

<div className="space-y-3">

<SectionCard variant="primary" title="은 / 는 — Topic Particles">

Used to introduce a topic or contrast something.

- Use **은** after a consonant (e.g., 학생은)
- Use **는** after a vowel (e.g., 나는)

</SectionCard>

<SectionCard variant="success" title="이 / 가 — Subject Particles">

Identifies the specific subject of the action.

- Use **이** after a consonant (e.g., 물이)
- Use **가** after a vowel (e.g., 사과가)

</SectionCard>

<SectionCard variant="warning" title="을 / 를 — Object Particles">

Marks the "object" of the sentence (the thing being acted upon).

- Use **을** after a consonant (e.g., 물을)
- Use **를** after a vowel (e.g., 차를)

</SectionCard>

</div>
